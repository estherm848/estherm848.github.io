Esther Mafouta March

About Her

Esther Mafouta March is freshman at Columbia University who served as President of the San Clemente Cool 2 Be Kind Club for the past two years. Throughout the past few years she has also worked with NAPAB, the National Association of People Against Bullying, in advocating for more comprehensive anti-bullying policies and presented a new anti-bullying bill to a representative of the California state legislature. Furthermore, this past summer she, along with four other women,founded CUSD Against Racism which is a coalition of current and former students that got together in the wake of the Black Lives Matter movement to demand anti-racist reform from Capistrano Unified School District (CUSD). She looks forward to furthering her social justice work through these two organizations and to continue paving the way for others to join in and do their part as well.

Professional Experience

CUSD Against Racism

Capistrano Unified School District Against Racism is a coalition of current and former students that came together in the wake of the Black Lives Matter movement to demand anti-racist reform from Capistrano Unified School District (CUSD). Over the course of the past few month we have:

  • Collected over 850 personal accounts of racism in CUSD schools and received nearly 2,000 signatures on our Open Letter Organized a rally and spoke at five District Board meetings with testimonies of racism and proposals for change Engaged thousands of community members on our Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, and website, Created task forces of students and teachers dedicated to combating racism at each high school Worked with students, teachers, CUSD PTSA, the teacher's union, the Cultural Proficiency Task Force and Executive Cabinet, members on the Board of Trustees, and more to share our experiences and brainstorm ways the District can create more positive learning environments for students of all racial and ethnic backgrounds
